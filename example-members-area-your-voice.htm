 Spring 2015 Summer 2015  Autumn 2015  Winter 2015
Never before in a general election campaign has mental health had such a high profile. Politicians have been fighting to push for change. While we are thrilled by the promises, we will have to work hard after the election to ensure these promises produce real change. Why are psychiatric hospitals still a trap? You’ve been put on a section. Suddenly everything in your life is decided for you. From your everyday schedule, to a prediction of where you’ll still be up to a year in your future, everything is pre-approved and assessed constantly. Personalised medicine in schizophrenia Dr James MacCabe is heading up a new 5 million pound study into the effects of glutamate chemical in the brain with a view to creating more personalised medicine. We spoke to him about what it could mean for the future. 
 The Challenges I faced when applying for work capability assessment (WCA) Denise describes the stressful process of applying for ESA and WCA which has had an adverse effect on her mental health. My Voice: Making your mark. Have you ever wondered how to influence the work of Rethink Mental Illness? How to meet other people in the same boat and actually do something to change the way things are? Three members tell us why they joined a Regional Committee and how it changed everything... Vitamin D and Psychosis – is there a link? New research suggests Vitamin D might be good for the brain and even linked to psychosis. A new study headed by Dr Fiona Gaughran is about to find out. 
 Could antibiotics help treat psychosis? Dr Paola Dazzan reveals new research that could potentially advance our understanding and treatment of mental illness. Raising your voice MPs are listening to our #FairFunding campaign. Over 3,250 of you contacted your MPs calling for fair mental health funding with some of you even meeting your MPs face to face!  Worried about welfare? The government is making changes to the welfare system through the Welfare Reform and Work Bill. We are monitoring this and are currently working out the best way we can ensure that the welfare system is supportive for those with mental health problems. Special Report: prison suicides We explain how we are working to help reduce self-harm and suicide rates in prison.. 
 Can a “Brain Training” app improve life for those with schizophrenia? A groundbreaking study led by Professor Barbara Sahakian at the University of Cambridge reveals that a new game could help minimise the impact of schizophrenia on everyday life. A look back on 2015 It has been a busy year for the campaigns team with more of our campaigners taking action than ever before, a General Election and the announcement of the Mental Health Taskforce, responsible for developing a five year strategy for mental health in England. We have pulled together some of the biggest moments from 2015. Introducing the new chair of the board of trustees, Philippa Lowe. At November’s National Members’ Day we welcomed our new Chair Philippa Lowe. Philippa is also the carer of her son Dan who has schizophrenia. Here she explains her longstanding connection to the charity and shares her hope for the future. National Members’ Day. 






 




 Spring 2014 Summer 2014 Autumn 2014  Winter 2014
Russell Kane, Rethink’s new ambassador for 2014, talks to Jonny Benjamin. Russell Kane is a multi-talented performer and writer and he talks to Your Voice about why he decided to become an ambassador for Rethink, the role of mental health education in our schools, and the potential that comedy could have in breaking down stigma. The Immune System and the Brain. Oxford University scientist and senior lecturer in psychiatry, Belinda Lennox is carrying out research on whether the immune system causes schizophrenia. After I’m Gone. Philippa Lowe discusses how to set up a trust or Will.
 Finding Mike Six years ago Jonny Benjamin, who had recently been diagnosed with schizoaffective disorder, went to Waterloo bridge to take his life. A stranger, who he nicknamed “Mike”, stopped him. At the beginning of this year Jonny launched a campaign to find “Mike” (whose name turned out to be Neil).  On 29 May, we released a film based on this search, to raise awareness about suicide. Here Jonny and Neil talk about their reunion and the making of the film. It’s the little things.... How two friends learnt to talk about depression. Mind the Gap National Psychosis Summit Special.
 Our Work to change the Criminal Justice System Research suggests that the percentage of prisoners who suffer from a mental health condition, such as personality disorder, depression, anxiety and psychosis, is far higher than the figure for the general public. We describe how we have worked with people affected by the criminal justice system and their families to improve their treatment.  An ongoing crisis Adequate housing is a fundamental for all of us, not least people with severe mental illness. Yet after 40 years of “Community Care”, society has failed to address the housing needs of this vulnerable group, especially those with chronic psychotic illnesses such as schizophrenia. 
 National Audit of Schizophrenia, Results are in! This autumn the National Audit of Schizophrenia published the results of its second audit. The audit, produced by the Royal College of Psychiatrist, looks at the quality of care and support people with schizophrenia and their carers receive from NHS community mental health services. Paula Reid gives us a summary of the findings. A Family Affair  Member of the year award winner Trina Whittaker, tells us why she’s proud to be a member and what she loved best about this year’s National Members’ Day. Voice Hearing around the world New research suggests attitudes to voice hearing differ dramatically depending on where you are in the world, with some cultures actually seeing them as “helpful”.
